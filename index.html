How it works & FAQ

How it works

  1. Set a plan. Pick a weekly or monthly budget of focus credits, a session length, and a money stake for the period.
  2. Show up inside the daily window. Credits are only claimable during your daily focus window — finish a focus session to claim one.
  3. Use it or lose it. Any of the day's share you don't claim before the window closes is forfeited for good, and unused credits at period end forfeit their share of the stake.

About Lapsr

Lapsr is a free online focus timer with a twist: instead of an open-ended pomodoro clock, you get a fixed budget of focus credits for the week or month, counting down in real time. Finish a focus session to claim a credit; whatever you don't spend before the period ends expires and forfeits part of a stake you set. It's built for deep work, studying, and anyone (ADHD included) who works better against a real, visible deadline than an infinite to-do list.

Is Lapsr free?

Yes — completely free, no account, no signup, no ads. It runs entirely in your browser and installs as an app (PWA) so it works offline.

How is it different from a normal Pomodoro timer?

A Pomodoro timer just counts 25-minute blocks. Lapsr wraps focus sessions in a metered budget: a set number of credits per week or month on a countdown, with a financial stake on the credits you waste — turning "use it or lose it" into real pressure to start.

Does my data leave my device?

No. Everything is stored locally in your browser. There is no server and no account. To move data to another device, export a JSON backup and import it there.

Can I use it for studying or ADHD focus?

Yes. The fixed period, live countdown, and forfeiture stake give the external structure that open-ended work lacks — useful for studying, deep work, and ADHD time-blindness.
